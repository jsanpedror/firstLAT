This theory emphasises the interaction between children and their care-givers.
According to this theory a child will learn more quickly with frequent interaction however children in all cultures pass through the same stages in acquiring language.  There are cultures in which adults do not adopt special ways of talking to children, so child directed speech may be useful but seems not to be essential.
